Hello! My name is Sofia Larson. I am a 17-year-old homeschool student with a passion for agriculture. I'm a proud member of Greensleeves 4-H, Wranglers 4-H, Skagit County Cattlemen's Association and the American Rabbit Breeders Association. This year, my 4-H projects include beef, rabbits, photography, gardening, bees, and veterinary science. Last year, I even showed swine. My goal is to earn a degree in agriculture or veterinary medicine. Ultimately, I want to become a cattle farmer.

I am excited to participate in my second Evergreen Youth Livestock Show and Sale at the Evergreen State Fairgrounds in Monroe. I will be showcasing and selling my market steer, Remi on August 23rd at 5 pm at the Fairgrounds in Monroe.

Remi is a grain-finished Angus/Maine Anjou market steer from Cindy Ovenell at Double O Ranch in Concrete, Washington. Since September, I have been working with Remi everyday, focusing on nutrition, halter training, grooming, walking, and showmanship. In March, Remi weighed approximately 1,300 pounds. The goal is for him to exceed 1,450 pounds by 16 months. He has access to haylage and timothy hay as well Purina wind & rain all season cattle mineral. He can access clean water at all times. Remi is on a carefully constructed grain feed plan, as well.

As a hands-on learner, this project has been invaluable for gaining practical farming experience. It has been a privilege to help with the calves and heifers on the farm as well as to be invited to help herd and observe during hoof trimming and veterinary care. Springherd Farms has taught me to treat cattle calmly and kindly, which not only benefits the steer but also contributes to better-tasting beef.

I have dedicated significant effort and fully funded this project on my own. I am reaching out to potential supporters to help offset the costs of feed, grooming, and training supplies, as well as the initial purchase price of the steer. Proceeds exceeding $4,000 from the sale will help fund my college education and also allow me to attend the "She's a Ranch Hand" Camp in Montana next summer, where I can gain even more hands-on experience in ranching.

I hope you will consider supporting my project, and if possible, attend the Livestock Show and Sale on August 23rd to bid on Remi. Your time and support mean the world to me, and I would be grateful for any assistance you can provide. I invite you to follow my journey on Instagram and Facebook at “Sofia and The Steer."

If you have any questions or would like more information, please contact me at sofiaandthesteer@gmail.com
Sincerely,
Sofia Larson
